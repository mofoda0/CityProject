About Tokyo

Tokyo, officially the Tokyo Metropolis, is the capital and most populous city in Japan. With a population of over 14 million in the city proper in 2023, it is one of the most populous urban areas in the world. The Greater Tokyo Area, which includes Tokyo and parts of six neighboring prefectures, is the most populous metropolitan area in the world, with 41 million residents as of 2024.

Lying at the head of Tokyo Bay, Tokyo is part of the Kantō region, on the central coast of Honshu, Japan's largest island. It is Japan's economic center and the seat of the Japanese government and the Emperor of Japan. The Tokyo Metropolitan Government administers Tokyo's central 23 special wards, which formerly made up Tokyo City; various commuter towns and suburbs in its western area; and two outlying island chains, the Tokyo Islands. Although most of the world recognizes Tokyo as a city, since 1943 its governing structure has been more akin to that of a prefecture, with an accompanying Governor and Assembly taking precedence over the smaller municipal governments that make up the metropolis. Special wards in Tokyo include Chiyoda, the site of the National Diet Building and the Tokyo Imperial Palace; Shinjuku, the city's administrative center; and Shibuya, a hub of commerce and business.

Famous Places

Tokyo is home to numerous famous places that attract tourists from around the world. Some of the most notable include:

  • Tokyo Tower: An iconic landmark offering panoramic views of the city.
  • Senso-ji Temple: Tokyo's oldest temple, located in Asakusa.
  • Shibuya Crossing: One of the busiest pedestrian crossings in the world.
  • Meiji Shrine: A serene Shinto shrine dedicated to Emperor Meiji and Empress Shoken.
  • Akihabara: A district famous for electronics, anime, and otaku culture.
  • Ueno Park: A spacious public park that houses museums, a zoo, and cherry blossom trees.

Famous foods

On our Secret Food Tour: Tokyo, you'll enjoy a variety of local Japanese dishes. Discover and taste the best Japanese cuisine in Tokyo.

  • Sushi: Freshly prepared raw fish, often served on vinegared rice (sushi) or on its own (sashimi).
  • Ramen: A noodle soup with various broths, toppings, and regional styles.
  • Tempura: Seafood or vegetables that are battered and deep-fried until crispy.
  • Yakitori: Grilled chicken skewers seasoned and cooked over a charcoal grill.
  • Soba: Buckwheat noodles that can be served hot or cold.
  • Onigiri: A rice ball stuffed with a variety of savory fillings and wrapped in seaweed.

Tokyo ticket prices vary by type: standard subway fares range from 180 to 330 yen per ride, while 24, 48, and 72-hour unlimited Tokyo Metro and Toei Subway passes cost 800, 1,200, and 1,500 yen for adults, respectively. Other tickets for specific attractions, such as the Tokyo Skytree, cost approximately 2,100 yen for the Tembo Deck or 3,100 yen for the combo ticket, while multi-attraction "Tokyo City Pass" options start around 3,900 yen.
